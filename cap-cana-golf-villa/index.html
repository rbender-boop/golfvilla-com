Why Cap Cana Is the World's Best Golf Villa Destination

Cap Cana occupies the northeastern tip of the Dominican Republic — a private gated community of approximately 30,000 acres that contains more concentrated golf and resort infrastructure than any comparable destination in the Caribbean. Inside a single resort perimeter: two Jack Nicklaus Signature golf courses, an 800-slip marina (the largest in the Caribbean), private beaches at Juanillo, a waterpark, the St. Regis Cap Cana hotel, Scape Park eco-adventure complex, and Villa Espada — an on-fairway private estate that cannot be found anywhere else in the hemisphere.

The resort operates on a model distinct from traditional Caribbean tourism. Cap Cana is privately owned and controlled by a single developer, which means the infrastructure quality — roads, landscaping, security, course conditioning — is maintained at a consistency level that resort hotel destinations cannot match. The community is gated and staffed 24 hours. The courses are maintained to ownership standards. The beaches are controlled and kept to a quality reserved for residents and guests.

For golf specifically, Cap Cana delivers something most of the world's golf destinations cannot: two independently world-ranked courses on the same property, playable in any order, on consecutive days, at member rates, with a private villa as your base. No single golf destination in Scotland, Ireland, Portugal, or anywhere in the continental United States offers this combination. That's the Cap Cana proposition, and it explains why groups that come once tend to return.

Punta Cana International Airport (PUJ) is 20 minutes by private transfer. Direct flights serve PUJ from virtually every major U.S. city, most Canadian cities, and seasonally from UK and European hubs. Miami is 2 hours. New York is 3.5 hours. Houston is 3.5 hours. Chicago is 4.5 hours. You can leave home on a Friday morning and be on the first tee at Punta Espada Friday afternoon.

Villa Espada sits on Fairway 5 of Punta Espada. Not adjacent to the resort. Not across a service road from the course. This is the physical fact that defines a golf villa, and Villa Espada embodies it completely.

This is where groups spend the hours between tee times — the space designed so that the non-golf hours are as good as the golf hours.

Cap Cana Golf — Two World-Class Courses

Punta Espada Golf Club — The Flagship

Jack Nicklaus designed Punta Espada in 2006, and it immediately set the standard for golf course design in the Caribbean and Latin America. Eighteen holes, par 72, playing from 5,320 to 7,132 yards depending on the tee selection. The course routing takes the player from the interior of the property outward toward the Caribbean Sea — and the back nine delivers some of the most dramatic oceanside golf in the world.

Nine holes (5, 6, 7, 8, 11, 12, 13, 14, and 17, per the course's public layout notes) are played alongside or directly over the Caribbean Sea. The tee shots on holes 6 and 7 require carries over sea inlets. The par-three 16th plays entirely over water. The stretch of holes 11 through 14 along the rocky shoreline is as visually striking as any run of holes on a resort course anywhere in the world.

GolfWeek Magazine has ranked Punta Espada the best resort course in Latin America and the Caribbean consistently across its ranking history. The course hosted the PGA Champions Tour Cap Cana Championship for multiple years, bringing the world's best senior professional golfers to this layout and verifying the course's caliber at the highest competitive level.

For Villa Espada guests, tee times are booked at owner-tier rates through the butler. The member rate at Punta Espada represents a meaningful discount versus public visitor pricing — for a group of eight playing four rounds over a week, the difference is substantial and partially offsets the villa rate itself.

Las Iguanas Golf Club — The New Course

Las Iguanas Golf Club opened in November 2025 and immediately changed the calculus for groups considering Cap Cana. A second Jack Nicklaus Signature design on the same property — this one incorporating a protected iguana nature reserve, cave formations, wetlands, and three consecutive oceanside holes that play along a stretch of rocky Caribbean coastline unlike anything else in the region.

Holes 12, 13, and 14 at Las Iguanas play along the Caribbean shoreline. The par-three 13th is the signature hole: a short iron over the open sea to a green perched at the cliff edge, with the iguana reserve visible from the tee. Golf writers who played the course in its opening weeks drew comparisons to the great Irish and Scottish links par-threes. That comparison will generate discussion — Las Iguanas is parkland in structure, not links — but the visual drama and the difficulty of the shot are undeniable.

For Villa Espada guests booking in 2026, Las Iguanas represents the opportunity to play a brand-new world-class Nicklaus design in its opening year, before it becomes widely established on international golf trip itineraries. The butler arranges member-rate tee times at Las Iguanas alongside Punta Espada as part of the standard villa booking.

Day Trip Courses from Cap Cana

The butler can also arrange day trips to courses outside Cap Cana. Casa de Campo is approximately 90 minutes by ground transfer and contains three Pete Dye-designed courses including Teeth of the Dog — consistently ranked the best golf course in the Caribbean by Golf Digest and a bucket-list destination for serious golfers. Puntacana Resort, 45 minutes from Cap Cana, contains Tom Fazio's Corales course (host of the PGA Tour Corales Puntacana Championship) and Pete Dye's La Cana, which plays 27 holes with 14 ocean-view holes. Both make for excellent day-trip additions to a Cap Cana villa stay, with transfers coordinated through the villa butler.

Beyond Golf — Cap Cana Experiences

Cap Cana is a complete resort destination, and the non-golf infrastructure within the resort is substantial. For groups with non-golfers, mixed-interest groups, or simply for rest days between rounds, the villa's included resort access covers a range of experiences within the gates.

Juanillo Beach & Eden Roc Beach Club

Juanillo is widely regarded as the finest beach within the Dominican Republic's top-tier resort corridor. The beach itself is natural and wide, with calm Caribbean water and minimal wave activity. Eden Roc at Cap Cana operates the beach club on this stretch of shoreline — sunbeds, food and beverage service, water sports equipment. Villa Espada guests access Juanillo as part of their stay.

Cap Cana Waterpark

The Cap Cana waterpark, which opened in 2025, is a resort amenity included in the Villa Espada stay. For groups with younger guests or families, it provides a full-day alternative to golf. For golf groups, it's a recovery-day option on rest days between rounds.

Cap Cana Marina — Deep-Sea Fishing & Yachts

The Cap Cana Marina is the largest marina in the Caribbean at approximately 800 slips. The butler can arrange deep-sea fishing charters departing from the marina — targeting blue marlin, white marlin, mahi-mahi, and wahoo in waters some of the most productive in the Caribbean. Half-day and full-day charters available. Yacht rentals for group cruises are also available through the marina.

Scape Park — Cenotes, Zip Lines & Eco-Adventure

Scape Park is a large eco-adventure complex within Cap Cana featuring cenote swimming, zip lines, ATV routes, and nature walking through the cave and wetland systems of the broader resort property. Popular with non-golf guests and as a half-day group excursion. The butler can arrange transportation and reservations.

St. Regis Cap Cana — Spa, Dining & Cigars

The St. Regis Cap Cana is the hotel anchor of the resort and its spa, restaurants, and cigar lounge are accessible to villa guests. The St. Regis spa is a full-service facility with treatment menus suited for post-round recovery. The hotel's restaurants provide a high-quality off-villa dining option for groups who want a formal dinner setting. The butler can arrange all reservations.

Getting to Cap Cana

From the United States

Punta Cana International Airport (PUJ) receives direct flights from virtually every major U.S. city. Approximate nonstop flight times: Miami (2 hrs), Atlanta (3 hrs), New York JFK/EWR (3.5 hrs), Houston IAH (3.5 hrs), Dallas DFW (4 hrs), Chicago ORD (4.5 hrs), Boston BOS (3.5 hrs), Philadelphia PHL (3.5 hrs), Los Angeles LAX (6.5 hrs). American, Delta, United, JetBlue, Southwest, and Spirit all serve PUJ with varying frequency by season.

From Canada

Toronto Pearson (YYZ) and Montreal Trudeau (YUL) are the primary Canadian departure points, with nonstops approximately 3.5–4 hours depending on origin. Air Canada, WestJet, Sunwing, and Air Transat provide regular service, with seasonal charter frequency increasing through winter months.

From the United Kingdom & Ireland

London Gatwick and Heathrow serve PUJ with approximately 9.5-hour nonstop or one-stop connections via U.S. gateways. Dublin connects via U.S. hubs, typically 10.5–11.5 hours total travel time. Seasonal direct charters operate from various UK regional airports during peak winter travel periods. British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and Thomas Cook/charter operators are the primary carriers.
