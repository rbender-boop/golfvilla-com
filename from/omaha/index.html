Why Omaha Golfers Choose Villa Espada

Omaha is one of America's most underappreciated golf cities. The Happy Hollow Club, Omaha Country Club, and the prestigious courses throughout eastern Nebraska and western Iowa serve a membership base anchored by Berkshire Hathaway, Mutual of Omaha, and the broader insurance and investment management industry. The city has disproportionate wealth relative to its size, and Omaha golf groups are well-organized, financially capable of premium trips, and historically loyal to their annual trip traditions. Cap Cana at 5.5 hours via connections is the kind of credentialed international golf trip that works for the Omaha market.

Punta Espada delivers something no domestic course can replicate: a Jack Nicklaus Signature ranked #1 in Latin America and the Caribbean, nine holes along the Caribbean Sea, played at member rates from a private villa on the 5th fairway. Las Iguanas Golf Club — the Nicklaus Signature opened November 2025 — adds a second world-class course with three oceanside holes.

The Golf at Cap Cana

Punta Espada Golf Club: Jack Nicklaus Signature, opened 2006. 18 holes, par 72. Ranked #1 in Latin America and the Caribbean by GolfWeek for over a decade. Nine holes along the Caribbean Sea. Host of the PGA Champions Tour Cap Cana Championship. Villa Espada sits on Fairway 5 — tee times at owner rates.

Las Iguanas Golf Club: Jack Nicklaus Signature, opened November 2025. Three oceanside holes at 12, 13, and 14. Member access included at owner rates.

Day trip option: Teeth of the Dog at Casa de Campo (Pete Dye, ranked #1 Caribbean by Golf Digest) is 90 minutes by private transfer. Butler-arranged.
